# Traveller — Frequently Asked Questions

## Booking & Payments

**How do I book a trip?**
You can book a trip by contacting us via WhatsApp at +91-98765-43210 or emailing us at bookings@traveller.com. We will send you a booking form and itinerary for confirmation.

**How much advance payment is required?**
A 30% advance payment is required to confirm your booking. The remaining 70% must be paid at least 7 days before the trip departure date.

**What payment methods do you accept?**
We accept UPI (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm), NEFT/IMPS bank transfer, and cash. We do not accept credit/debit cards at this time.

**Is GST included in the price?**
All quoted prices are inclusive of 5% GST.

**Can I get a custom/private trip?**
Yes! We offer customized itineraries for private groups of 4 or more. Contact us for pricing and availability.

## Cancellation & Refund Policy

**What is your cancellation policy?**
- Cancellation 30+ days before departure: 90% refund (10% processing fee)
- Cancellation 15–29 days before departure: 50% refund
- Cancellation 7–14 days before departure: 25% refund
- Cancellation less than 7 days before departure: No refund
- No-show on departure day: No refund

**What if Traveller cancels the trip?**
If we cancel a trip due to unforeseen circumstances (natural disasters, government restrictions, road closures), we offer a 100% refund or free rescheduling to the next available date.

**Can I reschedule my trip?**
One free reschedule is allowed if requested at least 15 days before departure. A second reschedule incurs a ₹1,000 administration fee.

**What happens if the weather forces a change in itinerary?**
We reserve the right to modify routes for safety. Refunds are not provided for weather-related itinerary changes, but we will do our best to provide an equivalent experience.

## What's Included & Excluded

**What is typically included in trip prices?**
- Transportation (pickup/drop from designated city, all internal transfers)
- Accommodation (guesthouses, homestays, or tents as specified)
- Meals as mentioned in the specific trip itinerary
- A certified trek/tour guide
- First-aid kit
- All permits and entry fees as specified

**What is typically NOT included?**
- Personal expenses (tips, laundry, phone calls, souvenirs)
- Travel insurance (strongly recommended — we can suggest providers)
- Any meals not mentioned in the itinerary
- Medical expenses
- Costs due to unforeseen route changes
- Personal trekking gear (poles, sleeping bags for high-altitude trips)

**Do you provide trekking gear?**
We provide basic group gear (ropes, first-aid, tents for camping trips). Personal gear like trekking poles, sleeping bags, and raincoats are the traveller's responsibility. We can arrange rentals at extra cost in some locations.

## Fitness & Health

**How fit do I need to be for the treks?**
- Kasol–Kheerganga, Bir Billing: Suitable for beginners; moderate walking fitness required.
- Chandrashila, Kedarnath: Moderate difficulty; prior walking/hiking experience helpful.
- Spiti Valley, Manali–Leh: Not a trek but involves long drives at high altitude; basic good health required.
- All participants should be able to walk 10–15km per day on trek days.

**Are there any age restrictions?**
- Minimum age: 12 years (children must be accompanied by a guardian)
- Senior travellers (60+) are welcome; please consult with us about your specific health conditions
- We recommend a doctor's clearance for anyone with heart conditions, respiratory issues, or recent surgeries

**What about altitude sickness?**
Altitude sickness (AMS) is a real risk on Spiti, Leh-Ladakh, and high-altitude treks. We advise:
- Acclimatize gradually; do not rush ascent.
- Drink plenty of water.
- Avoid alcohol on first few days.
- Carry Diamox (acetazolamide) if prescribed by your doctor.
- Our guides are trained to recognize and respond to AMS symptoms.

## Logistics & Practical Info

**What should I pack?**
A detailed packing list is sent to all confirmed participants. General essentials:
- Warm layers (even in summer for high-altitude trips)
- Waterproof jacket/poncho
- Sturdy trekking shoes (ankle support recommended)
- Sunscreen SPF 50+, sunglasses, lip balm
- Personal medications and a basic first-aid kit
- Power bank and torch
- Valid government-issued photo ID (mandatory for permits)
- Cash (ATMs are rare in remote areas)

**Is travel insurance mandatory?**
Travel insurance is strongly recommended but not mandatory. We advise coverage for trip cancellation, medical emergencies, and evacuation. We are not responsible for expenses arising from medical emergencies.

**Do you accommodate solo travellers?**
Yes! Solo travellers are welcome on all group departures. You will be grouped with other travellers. Single occupancy rooms are available at an additional cost (typically ₹500–₹1,500 extra per night).

**How do I get to the meeting/departure point?**
Departure details (exact meeting point, time, what to bring) are sent via WhatsApp 48 hours before the trip. Most trips depart from a central location in Delhi, Chandigarh, or Haridwar.
