# Traveller — Destinations Guide

## Spiti Valley
Spiti Valley is a cold desert mountain valley in the Himachal Pradesh district of Spiti. Located at an average altitude of 3,800m, it is one of the most remote and least-populated regions of India. The name "Spiti" means "The Middle Land" — the land between India and Tibet.

**Key Places:**
- **Kaza**: The administrative headquarters and base town of Spiti. Has ATMs, basic restaurants, and markets.
- **Key Monastery**: A Tibetan Buddhist monastery at 4,166m, one of the oldest in the region, perched on a hilltop.
- **Hikkim**: Home to the world's highest post office at 4,400m. You can send postcards from here.
- **Kibber**: One of the highest inhabited villages with a motorable road in the world.
- **Chandratal Lake**: A crescent-shaped high-altitude lake at 4,300m, known for crystal-clear water and stunning reflections.
- **Tabo Monastery**: Over 1,000 years old, called the "Ajanta of the Himalayas" for its ancient murals and stucco sculptures.
- **Pin Valley National Park**: Home to the rare snow leopard and Siberian ibex.
- **Dhankar Monastery**: A 1,000-year-old monastery precariously perched on a cliff at 3,890m.

**Travel Tips:**
- Requires Inner Line Permit (ILP) — Traveller arranges this for all guests.
- Altitude sickness is common; acclimatize properly and carry Diamox if prescribed.
- Limited ATMs — carry enough cash from Shimla or Manali.
- Mobile connectivity is sparse; BSNL works in some areas.
- Roads can be blocked by landslides; plan for flexibility.

## Manali
Manali is a high-altitude Himalayan resort town in Himachal Pradesh, situated at 2,050m in the Beas River valley. It is a gateway to Lahaul-Spiti and Leh-Ladakh and a popular base for trekking, skiing, and adventure sports.

**Key Places:**
- **Rohtang Pass (3,978m)**: A high mountain pass connecting Manali to the Lahaul-Spiti Valley. Requires a permit (₹550 for petrol vehicles).
- **Solang Valley**: Known for skiing in winter and paragliding in summer; 14km from Manali.
- **Hadimba Temple**: A 16th-century wooden temple dedicated to goddess Hadimba, set amidst cedar forests.
- **Old Manali**: Quaint village with cafés, guesthouses, and hippie culture.
- **Vashisht Village**: Hot sulphur springs and the ancient Vashisht Temple.
- **Beas Kund Trek**: A moderate 2-day trek to the glacial source of the Beas River.

**Travel Tips:**
- Well-connected by road from Delhi (~12–14 hours by bus) and Chandigarh.
- Season: March–June (spring/summer), December–February (snow).
- Avoid Rohtang Pass during monsoon (July–August) due to landslide risk.

## Kasol
Kasol is a small hamlet in the Parvati Valley, Himachal Pradesh, situated on the banks of the Parvati River at 1,580m. It is a popular backpacker destination known as the "Amsterdam of India" for its relaxed atmosphere and large Israeli traveller community.

**Key Places:**
- **Kheerganga**: A 12–14km trek from Barshaini, leading to a meadow at 2,960m with natural hot-water springs. Overnight camping available.
- **Chalal**: A forested village across the river from Kasol, connected by a bridge; popular for riverside walks.
- **Manikaran**: A religious town 4km from Kasol with hot springs and the Manikaran Sahib Gurudwara (Sikh pilgrimage site). The langar (free community kitchen) feeds thousands daily.
- **Tosh Village**: A scenic village at the end of a side valley, popular for its views and slower pace.
- **Malana**: An isolated village with a unique culture and restricted photography — known for its ancient customs.

**Travel Tips:**
- Nearest town with ATM is Bhuntar (30km); carry cash.
- Kheerganga trek requires a day or overnight — carry trekking shoes.
- Avoid Malana if you are not respectful of local customs (no touching walls, residents, etc.).

## Bir Billing
Bir is a small town in the Kangra district of Himachal Pradesh, known as the paragliding capital of India and one of the top paragliding sites in the world. Billing (1,900m) is the takeoff site; Bir (1,400m) is the landing zone.

**Key Places:**
- **Billing Takeoff Site**: World-class paragliding launch site; international competitions are held here.
- **Tibetan Colony**: Bir has a significant Tibetan refugee settlement with authentic monasteries, cafés, and handicraft shops.
- **Palpung Sherabling Monastery**: A major Tibetan Buddhist monastery known for its giant golden Buddha statue.
- **Deer Park Institute**: Cultural centre hosting talks, workshops, and retreats.

**Travel Tips:**
- Best flying conditions: March–May and September–November.
- Monsoon (July–August) is generally not suitable for paragliding.
- Nearest airport: Kangra (Gaggal) Airport, ~60km away.

## Kedarnath
Kedarnath is a Hindu temple town in the Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand, located at 3,583m in the Garhwal Himalayan range. The Kedarnath Temple is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Shiva and part of the Char Dham pilgrimage.

**Key Places:**
- **Kedarnath Temple**: Ancient stone temple believed to be over 1,000 years old, rebuilt by Adi Shankaracharya. The temple opens in May (Akshaya Tritiya) and closes in November (Diwali period).
- **Vasuki Tal**: A high-altitude glacial lake at 4,135m, accessible by a 6km trek from Kedarnath; requires a fit trekker.
- **Gaurikund**: The base camp and trailhead for the 18km trek to Kedarnath. Has hot sulphur springs.
- **Bhairavnath Temple**: Just above Kedarnath temple; offers panoramic views of the valley.
- **Sonprayag**: Road-end point for private vehicles; government jeeps/shared taxis run to Gaurikund.

**Travel Tips:**
- The trek from Gaurikund to Kedarnath is 18km one-way; ponies and palanquins are available.
- Helicopter services operate from Phata and Guptkashi — must be pre-booked.
- Carry warm clothes even in summer; temperatures drop sharply at night.
- Online registration (biometric) is mandatory for Kedarnath Yatra on the official Uttarakhand tourism portal.
